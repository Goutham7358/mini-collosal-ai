Problem Statement & Motivation

Training modern LLMs (100M–billions of parameters) requires distributing computation across
multiple GPUs
Multiple parallelism strategies exist (DP, TP, PP, ZeRO) — each with different communication
patterns and memory trade-offs
The right strategy depends on both the hardware topology and the model architecture
What we did:
1
Built all four strategies from scratch using torch.distributed primitives
2
Composed them into hybrid configurations through a unified 3D plugin
3
Studied how interconnect speed and axis-to-link mapping affect throughput
4
Tested across two architectures (GPT-2 and T5) to isolate architectural effects

Key Findings
1

Placement delivers 17–27% throughput gain
Keeping bandwidth-heavy axes (DP, TP) on the fast intra-node link consistently outperforms the
reverse, across all models.

2

DP overtakes TP as the placement bottleneck at low TP degrees
Standard expectation: TP is most sensitive (all-reduce every layer). At TP=2, individual TP
messages are small (KB–MB) and TCP handles them fine. DP gradient sync (∼1 GB+) saturates
the slow link. At higher TP degrees (4, 8), TP would become the bottleneck again.

3

Encoder-decoder models amplify the placement penalty
T5 sees +27% gain from good placement vs +20% for GPT-2. T5’s extra cross-attention
all-reduces make it more communication-bound, so slow-link overhead hits harder.

4

Symmetric vs asymmetric architectures: 30–46% scaling gap
GPT-2’s identical blocks give balanced pipeline stages and fewer all-reduces. T5’s encoder-decoder
split creates 54% memory imbalance and 25% more TP communication.

Phase 3 — Group 1: Single-Node PCIe Baselines (4× T4, g4dn.12xlarge)
======================================================================
Model: GPT-2 Medium (24L, 354M params) for all experiments
Hardware: 1× g4dn.12xlarge, 4× Tesla T4 16GB, PCIe intra-node

Experiment  | Config     | Throughput   | Peak Mem  | Phase 2 (TCP) | Speedup
------------|------------|--------------|-----------|---------------|--------
1a. Baseline| Single GPU | 1,399 tok/s  | 7.67 GB   | 1,361 tok/s   | 1.0x
1b. DP(4)   | DP 4-way   | 3,389 tok/s* | 7.67 GB   | 1,393 tok/s*  | 2.4x
1c. TP(4)   | TP 4-way   | 2,518 tok/s  | 3.38 GB   | 959 tok/s     | 2.6x
1d. PP(4)   | PP 1F1B    | 3,200 tok/s  | 2.77 GB   | 3,070 tok/s   | 1.04x
1e. ZeRO-1  | DP+ZeRO-1  | 2,832 tok/s* | 9.47 GB   | 934 tok/s*    | 3.0x

* = aggregate throughput across all GPUs

Key findings:
- DP: 60.6% scaling efficiency on PCIe vs 25.6% on TCP (2.4x improvement)
- TP: 2.6x faster on PCIe — PCIe handles the 48 per-layer all-reduces much better
- PP: Only 4% faster — PP uses small point-to-point sends, not bandwidth-bound
- ZeRO-1: 3.0x faster — all-gather/reduce-scatter much faster on PCIe
- PP still has highest throughput (3,200 tok/s) even on PCIe

Phase 3 — Group 5: Multi-Model Placement Comparison (8 GPUs, dp=4, tp=2)
==========================================================================
Config: dp=4, tp=2, pp=1 (no PP) — isolates DP vs TP placement question
Hardware: 2× g4dn.12xlarge (4× T4 each), PCIe intra-node, TCP inter-node

Good placement: TP intra-node (PCIe), DP inter-node (TCP)
Bad placement:  TP inter-node (TCP), DP intra-node (PCIe)

Model          | Params | Good (tok/s) | Bad (tok/s) | Delta  | Winner
---------------|--------|-------------|-------------|--------|--------
GPT-2 Small    | 117M   | 5,473       | 5,594       | +2.2%  | Bad
GPT-2 Medium   | 354M   | 2,288       | 2,315       | +1.2%  | ~Tie
GPT-2 Large    | 774M   | 2,230       | 2,413       | +8.2%  | Bad
GPT-2 XL       | 1.5B   | OOM         | OOM         | —      | N/A (>16GB)

Analysis:
"Bad" placement (DP on fast link) wins or ties for ALL tested model sizes.

This occurs because with dp=4 and tp=2, the DP gradient volume always exceeds
the TP activation volume:

Model   | DP grad volume     | TP activation volume | Ratio (TP/DP)
--------|--------------------|--------------------- |----------
Small   | 117M/2 × 4 = 234MB| 12×2×4×256×768×4=60MB| 0.26
Medium  | 354M/2 × 4 = 708MB| 24×2×4×256×1024×4=201MB| 0.28
Large   | 774M/2 × 4= 1548MB| 36×2×4×256×1280×4=377MB| 0.24

DP gradient sync dominates communication in all cases, so placing DP on the
fast link (PCIe) always helps more than placing TP there.

The larger the model, the bigger the absolute difference (Large: 8.2% vs Small: 2.2%)
because the absolute DP gradient volume grows, making the TCP-vs-PCIe gap more impactful.

Implication: For models on our T4 hardware (16GB, PCIe ~15GB/s, TCP ~0.6GB/s),
the conventional "TP on fast link" heuristic is suboptimal. A cost-based
auto-parallelism configurator would discover the better placement automatically.

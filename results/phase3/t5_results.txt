Multi-Model Experiment Results: GPT-2 vs T5
=============================================

Goal: Show that hybrid parallelism benefits differ across model architectures.
      GPT-2 (decoder-only, symmetric) scales better than T5 (encoder-decoder,
      asymmetric) under the same hybrid parallelism configuration and hardware.

Hardware: 2× g4dn.12xlarge (4× T4 per node, PCIe intra-node ~15 GB/s,
          TCP inter-node ~0.6 GB/s)
Config:   dp=2, pp=2, tp=2 on 8 GPUs

=== Models ===

  GPT-2 Small:  12 decoder layers, hidden=768,  8 heads, ~117M params
  T5-base:      12 encoder + 12 decoder layers, hidden=768, 12 heads, ~237M params
  GPT-2 Medium: 24 decoder layers, hidden=1024, 16 heads, ~345M params

=== Hybrid Parallelism Speedup Over Single-GPU Baseline ===

  Model          | Params | Single GPU  | Hybrid 8-GPU | Speedup
  GPT-2 Small    | 117M   | 3,879 tok/s | 8,678 tok/s  | 2.24×
  T5-base        | 237M   | 3,359 tok/s | 5,790 tok/s  | 1.72×
  GPT-2 Medium   | 345M   | 1,404 tok/s | 3,519 tok/s  | 2.51×

  KEY FINDING: Using the same hardware and same hybrid parallelism config,
  GPT-2 achieves 2.2–2.5× speedup while T5 achieves only 1.72×.
  Both GPT-2 variants scale similarly despite different sizes,
  confirming the difference is architectural, not size-related.

=== Why GPT-2 Scales Better ===

1. SYMMETRIC PIPELINE (PP):
   GPT-2 has identical transformer blocks → balanced pipeline stages.
   T5 encoder-decoder split creates load imbalance:
     - Encoder stage: 2.51 GB peak memory
     - Decoder stage: 3.87 GB peak memory (54% more)
   In the 1F1B schedule, the encoder completes each microbatch faster than
   the decoder and must wait every cycle for the decoder to catch up.
   These per-microbatch idle gaps accumulate into significant throughput loss.
   GPT-2 stages: 1.65 vs 1.71 GB (Small), 4.07 vs 4.08 GB (Medium) → balanced.

2. FEWER ALL-REDUCE OPERATIONS (TP):
   GPT-2 block = self-attn + MLP → 2 all-reduces per block
   T5 encoder block = self-attn + MLP → 2 all-reduces per block
   T5 decoder block = self-attn + CROSS-ATTN + MLP → 3 all-reduces per block
   Total per forward pass:
     GPT-2 Small (12 blocks):  12 × 2 = 24 all-reduces
     GPT-2 Medium (24 blocks): 24 × 2 = 48 all-reduces
     T5-base (12+12 blocks):   12 × 2 + 12 × 3 = 60 all-reduces (25% more than Medium!)
   More all-reduces = more TP communication overhead.

=== 8-GPU Placement Comparison (dp=2, pp=2, tp=2) ===

  Three placements tested — what goes on the slow inter-node TCP link:

                           | T5-base     | GPT-2 Medium
  Good  (PP inter-node)    | 5,790 tok/s | 3,519 tok/s
  TP inter-node            | 5,838 tok/s | 3,543 tok/s
  Bad   (DP inter-node)    | 4,558 tok/s | 2,926 tok/s

  Good/Bad speedup         | +27.0%      | +20.3%

  SURPRISING FINDING: TP inter-node shows NO penalty vs good placement!
  DP inter-node is the actual worst placement, not TP inter-node.

  Why: With TP=2, each all-reduce is between just 2 GPUs. Individual
  message sizes are small (few hundred KB to few MB per all-reduce).
  TCP at ~0.6 GB/s handles these fine despite high frequency.

  DP gradient sync, on the other hand, moves the ENTIRE model's gradients
  in one shot (~950 MB for T5, ~1.4 GB for GPT-2 Medium). This single
  massive bandwidth-bound transfer saturates the slow TCP link.

  This means the correct placement heuristic is:
    → Put the BANDWIDTH-HEAVY axis on the fast link (DP on PCIe)
    → The LATENCY-SENSITIVE axis (TP) tolerates TCP at small TP degrees
    → PP has minimal communication volume and tolerates any link

  GPT-2 Small good placement results (from previous experiments):
    Good: 8,546 tok/s  |  Bad (DP inter-node): 7,156 tok/s  |  +19.4%

=== Takeaways for Report ===

1. ARCHITECTURE DETERMINES HYBRID SCALING, NOT MODEL SIZE.
   GPT-2 (decoder-only) achieves 2.2–2.5× hybrid speedup.
   T5 (encoder-decoder) achieves only 1.72× with the same config.
   Two factors: pipeline imbalance from asymmetric stages, and
   25% more TP all-reduces from cross-attention.

2. PIPELINE IMBALANCE IN ENCODER-DECODER MODELS.
   T5 encoder stage (2.51 GB) vs decoder stage (3.87 GB) — 54% heavier.
   GPT-2 stages are near-identical (4.07 vs 4.08 GB).
   The encoder repeatedly waits for the slower decoder in the 1F1B
   schedule, wasting compute on every microbatch cycle.

3. DP IS THE MOST PLACEMENT-SENSITIVE AXIS (not TP at small degrees).
   DP inter-node: 17–27% throughput penalty (bandwidth-bound, huge volume).
   TP inter-node: ~0% penalty at TP=2 (many small messages, latency-bound
     but individual messages small enough for TCP).
   PP inter-node: ~0% penalty (tiny P2P activation sends).
   The correct heuristic is: keep DP on the fastest link.

4. PLACEMENT EFFECT IS STRONGER FOR T5 (+27%) THAN GPT-2 (+20%).
   T5's higher communication volume (cross-attention gradients in DP sync)
   makes it more sensitive to the DP placement decision.
